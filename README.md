# CSAT_Detailed-Business-Analysis-Report

## 🔹 Project Overview

Data was extracted from a customer support SQL database containing call records, SLA metrics, customer feedback, channels, and geographic information.

Built an end-to-end customer service analytics solution using SQL and Power BI.

Focused on customer satisfaction (CSAT), operational efficiency, support channels, billing issues, and service performance.

Created interactive dashboards to identify dissatisfaction drivers, monitor KPIs, and recommend business improvements.

## 🔹 Tools & Technologies Used

SQL → Data extraction, joins, KPI calculations, trend analysis

Power BI → Dashboard creation, KPI monitoring, interactive visualization

Excel → Data cleaning and preprocessing (if applicable)

## 🔹 Key Features

CSAT score and customer satisfaction performance tracking

Call center operational analysis (volume, duration, SLA)

Support channel performance comparison

Billing issue identification and complaint analysis

Geographic analysis of support demand

Call trend forecasting for future capacity planning

## 🔹 Data Analysis Performed (SQL)

Analyzed total call volume, average call duration, SLA adherence, and CSAT score

Measured percentage of calls handled within SLA vs delayed calls

Segmented customer interactions by support channels

Identified top call reasons contributing to dissatisfaction

Tracked state-wise call distribution patterns

Analyzed historical call volume trends for demand forecasting

## 🔹 Dashboard Insights (Power BI)
### Customer Satisfaction Analysis

Average CSAT score: 2.05 / 5

Indicates widespread dissatisfaction with service experience

### Operational Performance

Total Calls: 1000

Average Call Duration: 25.15 mins

61% calls handled within SLA, 39% delayed

### Channel Analysis

Call Center is the dominant support channel (~33%)

Web/self-service channels underutilized

### Call Reason Analysis

Billing-related queries contribute nearly 70% of total calls

Major pain point affecting trust and satisfaction

### Geographic Analysis

Issues evenly spread across states

Suggests centralized/systemic operational problems

### Demand Trend Analysis

Call volume shows upward trend

Potential signal of growing customer base or unresolved recurring issues

## 🔹 Key Business Insights

Low customer satisfaction despite moderate SLA performance

Billing process is the biggest driver of support demand

Long call durations indicate inefficient issue resolution

High dependency on manual support channels increases cost

Rising call volume may impact future service quality

## 🔹 Business Recommendations

Optimize billing systems with clearer statements and proactive alerts

Improve agent training on communication and first-call resolution

Reduce call duration using knowledge base and standard workflows

Enhance chatbot, website, and self-service automation

Improve workforce planning and real-time SLA monitoring

Use forecasting models for staffing and capacity planning

## 🔹 Project Outcome

Enabled data-driven decision-making for customer service operations

Identified core causes of dissatisfaction and cost inefficiencies

Provided roadmap to improve CSAT, reduce support load, and optimize operations

Built a scalable dashboard solution for continuous customer experience monitoring
